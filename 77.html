Prerequisite Skills: Emergency airway management, Universal precautions

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Emergency airway management should tube become dislodged
  • Assessment of patient’s need for treatment
  • Protects patient privacy
  • Utilizes patient identifiers and safety practices
  • Universal precautions

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment
  • Confirm order
  • Administer pain medication if needed
  • Gather equipment: Clean gloves, trach kit, sterile gloves, goggles and mask
  • Ensure emergency equipment is at bedside: Bag-valve mask, oxygen, obturator from current trach, spare trash of the same side and one smaller, working suction

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide for privacy
  • Wash hands
  • Explain procedure. Agree on a method of communication is the patient has any difficulty breathing/pain.
  • Educate patient about reporting of discomfort

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  1. Assist patient to a semi-fowler’s position and place towel or pad across chest
  2. Bring over-bed table close.  Put trash receptacle at bedside.
  3. Assess respiratory rate, depth, O2 saturation and lung sounds
  4. Put on gown, goggles and mask along with gloves
  5. Remove oxygen source.
  6. Slowly remove site dressing, assessing drainage and skin.
  7. Replace oxygen source and remove gloves.
  8. Open sterile kit, apply sterile gloves.  Open cotton tipped cleaning applicator
  9. Remove oxygen source with non-dominate hand (or assistant)
  10. Use dominate hand to clean around stoma site with applicators.  Gently slide pre-cut 4 x 4 dressing under the faceplate.
  11. Reassess the patient respiratory rate, depth and saturation levels

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Infection Control)

  • Remove protective equipment
  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

Patient’s response to procedure including drainage amount and color.
