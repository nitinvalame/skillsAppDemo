Steps of the Procedure (Patient
Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assess IV site for the presence of infiltration or inflammation
  2. Clamp off primary line
  3. Clamp the extension tubing, if present.  Remove the primary IV tubing from the extension set and attach the lock or adapter device. 
  4. Cleanse the cap of the lock with alcohol wipe
  5. Unclamp the extension set and flush with saline per agency policy
  6. Reclamp the extension tubing and remove the syringe
  7. Use tape to secure the extension tubing

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assess IV site for the presence of infiltration or inflammation
  2. Clamp off primary line
  3. Prime the lock of adapter device and extension set with normal saline.  Clamp the extension set.
  4. Put on gloves
  5. Place a gauze 4 x 4 sponge underneath the IV connection hub between the IV catheter and tubing.
  6. Stabilize the hub of the IV catheter with nondominant hand.  Use dominant hand to quickly twist and disconnect IV tubing from the catheter.  Discard it. Attach the primed lock or adapter device to the IV catheter bub.
  7. Cleanse the cap with an alcohol wipe and unclamp the extension set.
  8. Flush the hub with saline per agency policy
  9. Remove gloves
  10. Tape lock or adapter device

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Assessment of insertion site
  • Document that IV fluids were discontinued
  • Patient’s response to procedure
