Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  1. Place patient in supine position with arms by his/her sides.  Raise head of bed if necessary for comfort.  Expose arms, legs and chest and drape appropriately.
  2. Select a flat fleshy area to place electrodes. Avoid muscular and bony areas.  Trim excessive hair, clean excess oil from skin
  3. Apply electrode pads to distal portion of each leg and arm. 
  4. Connect limb lead wires to the pads.  Note: RA and LA would be placed on the right arm and left arms; RL and LL would be placed on the right and left legs
  5. Apply electrode pads to chest, in the following locations:
    1. V1: 4th intercostal space, right sternal border
    2. V2: 4th intercostal space, left sternal border
    3. V3: Halfway between V2 and V4
    4. V4: 5th intercostal space , midclavicular line
    5. V5: 5th intercostal space, anterior axillary line
    6. V6: 5th intercostal space, midaxillary line, level with V4
  6. Ask the patient to breathe normally and relax
  7. Press the start button, run and observe tracing quality

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  1. Place patient in supine position with arms by his/her sides.  Raise head of bed if necessary for comfort.  Expose chest and drape appropriately.
  2. Select a flat fleshy area to place electrodes. Avoid muscular and bony areas.  Trim excessive hair, clean excess oil from skin
  3. Apply electrode pads to chest in the following locations:
    1. Right arm (RA) (white) directly below the clavicle near the right shoulder
    2. Left arm (LA) (black) directly below the clavicle near the left shoulder
    3. Right leg (RL) (green) on right iliac fossa of right lower abdomen
    4. Left leg (LL) (red) on left iliac fossa of left lower abdomen
  4. Observe tracing quality

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Infection Control)

  • Remove leads and pads
  • Clean the patient skin
  • Disconnect the wires from the pad, dispose of pads and clean machine

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Patient’s response to procedure including outcome of reading
