Critical Element Criteria can be applied to any procedure.  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Supplies for site cleansing and dressing changes should be single-use
  • Maintain aseptic technique throughout
  • Perform hand hygiene before and after procedure
  • Wear clean or sterile gloves (additional precaution per Infusion Nursing Society includes use of facemasks and sterile gloves)
  • Remove existing dressing and inspect the site visually, noting normal or abnormal findings
  • Apply antiseptic to the site using >0.5% chlorhexidine preparation with alcohol; tincture of iodine, an iodophor, or 70% alcohol  
  • Do not apply topical antibiotic ointment or creams to catheter site
  • Cover with either sterile gauze or sterile, transparent, semipermeable dressing
  • Verbalized to patient before steps that may cause discomfort

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment
  • Confirm order
  • Gather equipment: Dressing change kit, clean gloves, disposable mask for patient, chlorhexidine-impregnated sponge per protocol

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide for privacy
  • Explain procedure
  • Confirm allergies
  • Wash hands

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assist the patient to the proper positioning depending on the type/location of the central venous catheter.
  2. Have patient turn head to side, opposite of central line.  Apply disposable mask.
  3. Open sterile kit and remove the mask, not contaminating the kit.  Put on mask.
  4. Put on clean gloves.
  5. Remove old dressing (and antiseptic patch) without dislodging the catheter.  Remove from outside working toward the catheter, securing so it is not dislodged.
  6. Properly remove gloves with soiled dressing inside.  Dispose of both.
  7. Assess insertion site.
  8. Remove sterile gloves from kit without contaminating kit, placing gloves on clean area of over bed table.  Open sterile chlorhexidine-impregnated sponge and place on sterile field, if recommended.  Apply sterile gloves
  9. Grasp chlorhexidine preparation and activate. Scrub stating at insertion site and working outward until an area is cleaned larger than the new dressing.  Wipe catheter and hub from insertion site, working distally.  Allow to dry for greater than 1 minute.
  10. Cover site with sterile chlorhexidine-impregnated sponge and transparent dressing.
  11. Remove gloves, label dressing with date, time, initials, placing the label on the edge of the dressing.
  12. Dispose of supplies, removing mask from the patient.

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Assessment of insertion site
  • Patient’s response to procedure
