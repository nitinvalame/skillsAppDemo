Prerequisite Skills: Assessment skills, Vital Signs, Aseptic Technique

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Understanding a blood and blood products
  • Assessment of patient’s need for treatment
  • Monitors for reaction
  • Maintains universal precautions
  • Protects patient privacy
  • Utilizes patient identifiers and safety practices

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment (review of recent laboratory findings)
  • Confirm order
  • Gather equipment: Blood product, administration tubing, 0.9% sodium chloride solution, IV pole, IV catheter (20 gauge or larger), disposable gloves, tape, saline flush (if needed)

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment (review of recent laboratory findings)
  • Confirm order

Gather equipment: Blood product, administration tubing, 0.9% sodium chloride solution, IV pole, IV catheter (20 gauge or larger), disposable gloves, tape, saline flush (if needed)Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide for privacy
  • Inquire about history of transfusions or transfusion reactions
  • Explain procedure and obtain consent
  • Confirm allergies
  • Wash hands
  • Obtain baseline assessment (including heart and lung signs, urine output and skin condition)
  • Assessment and confirmation of placement if IV catheter
  • Educate patient about reporting of chills, itching, rash, or unusual symptoms.
  • Premedicate (if ordered)

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control, Safety)

  1. Hang 0.9% NaCl with blood administration set, priming tubing, to initiate IV infusion
  2. Start IV flowing with sodium chloride solution after site patency confirmed
  3. Obtain blood product from blood bank according to agency policy
  4. Complete identification and check as per required by agency (identification number, blood group and type, expiration date, patient’s name, etc.)
  5. Confirm identification with a 2nd registered nurse
  6. Inspect blood bag for clots or any abnormalities
  7. Obtain baseline set of vital signs before beginning transfusion
  8. Spike the blood bag and prime with blood, begin infusion slowly (no more then 25-50 mL for the first 15 minutes.
  9. Stay with the patient for the first 15 minutes to monitor for reactions
  10. Re-assess vital signs at least every 15 minutes for the first half hour, following the institutions recommendations.
  11. Continue to reassess for reaction throughout the transfusion

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Once blood is complete, clamp off blood tubing and open sodium chloride infusion to flush blood tubing.
  • Once tubing flushed of blood, return IV site to order state. 
  • Discard bag and tubing per institution policy
  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Patient’s response to procedure including reaction, completion vital signs, intake volume
