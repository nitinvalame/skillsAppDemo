Prerequisite Skills: Communication

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Define terms of safety including body mechanics, alignment, posture, balance, and body support
  • Identify essential principles for safe and efficient body mechanics
  • Correctly demonstrate the ability to utilize proper body mechanics with all nursing activities
  • Maintain universal precautions

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Gather appropriate assessment equipment (additional assistance, gait belt, slide board, lift sheet, wheel chair)
  • Consider conditions that affect moving or transferring (obesity, fragility, amputation, paralysis, altered level of consciousness, language barriers, hearing and vision loss)

Procedure: (Infection Control, Safety)

  1. Wash hands before coming in contact with patient
  2. Explain the procedure to the patient, verify understanding
  3. Use stronger, larger muscles to perform tasks which require physical effort
  4. When moving a heavy object, push or pull rather than lift
  5. Get help if object is too heavy to lift
  6. Lift in a smooth motion to prevent injury
  7. Maintain good posture
  8. Avoid twisting the body
  9. Bend knees, keep back strait, spread feet apart and use leg muscles when lifting

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Confirm patient is safe with call light within reach before leaving room
