Prerequisite Skills: Nutritional Assessment

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Confirm placement of tube
  • Check for residual
  • Holds feeding for excessive residual
  • Keeps bed positioned to minimize risk of aspiration

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care)

  1. Assist the patient to the proper positioning with head of bed elevated.
  2. Apply clean gloves and unclamp the tube
  3. Confirm placement of tube as appropriate for the type of tube.
    1. For nasogastric tube check placement with air auscultation
    2. For nasojejunal tube check placement by measuring length of the tube from the nose to rube hub.  Compare to previous placement
    3. Placement check not needed for gastrostomy tube
  4. Check for residual if indicated.  Attach syringe and pull back on plunger.  Note amount and character of residual.  Replace residual per policy and flush tube with tap water.
  5. Remove plunger from syringe and attach syringe to the tube.
  6. Add feeding to syringe and administer feeding using gravity to control the rate of feeding.  Raising and lowering the syringe will help control the rate
  7. Flush the tubing with tap water
  8. Clamp the tube

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care)

  1. Assist the patient to the proper positioning with head of bed elevated.
  2. Apply clean gloves and pause the feeding and disconnect  the tube
  3. Confirm placement of tube as appropriate for the type of tube.
    1. For nasogastric tube check placement with air auscultation
    2. For nasojejunal tube check placement by measuring length of the tube from the nose to rube hub.  Compare to previous placement
    3. Placement check not needed for gastrostomy tube
  4. Check for residual if indicated.  Attach syringe and pull back on plunger.  Note amount and character of residual.  Replace residual per policy and flush tube with tap water.
  5. Place up to 4 hours of nutrition in bag.  Label with date and time. Change bag per policy.
  6. Hang feeding bag on IV pole. Prime tubing, connect to feeding pump and program at ordered rate of administration.
  7. Attach tubing to end of tube and begin pump

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Rinse the syringe with water, discard if expired per policy
  • Remove gloves
  • Keep head of bed elevated at least 30 minutes to prevent aspiration
  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Amount of residual, time of feeding, type and amount of feeding.
  • Patient’s response to procedure.
