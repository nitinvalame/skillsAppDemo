Prerequisite Skills: Universal Precautions, basic computer skills

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Use of guardrails for all medication administration
  • Proper cleaning of equipment before and after use
  • Safely troubleshoots pump problems
  • Double checks all programing functions before infusion
  • Takes pump out of circulation when malfunction identified

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Determine need for Smart Pump use based on medication being administered and agency policy
  • Gather equipment (pump, proper tubing, solution/medication for administration)

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Explain procedure
  • Confirm allergies
  • Wash hands

Steps of the Procedure (Infection Control, Safety)

  1. Confirm cleanliness of equipment before taking into patient room
  2. Plug in pump
  3. Prime solution and medication as per policy
  4. Turn on pump
  5. Program per pump instructions, using safety guardrails
  6. Double check pump settings before beginning infusion
  7. Monitor pump during infusion, safely troubleshoot any issues or alarms
    1. Air in line
    2. Distal occlusion
    3. Proximal occlusion
    4. Remove pump from circulation if determined to be malfunctioning

After Procedure (Infection Control, Safety)

  • Clean equipment per agency policy
  • Wash hands before leaving room.
