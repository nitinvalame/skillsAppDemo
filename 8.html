Pain

Prerequisite Skills:

Concepts of Communication

Critical Element Criteria:

Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Understanding of the importance and variations that can occur with pain management

Procedure for Nurse to Health Care Provider

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Review facility protocol and health care provider’s orders for pain assessment
  • Review previous pain assessment and treatments

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide privacy
  • Accommodate for limitations in communication

Ask the Following Questions: (Patient Centered Care)

  1. Are you hurting, have pain or discomfort?
    P – Provoking Factors
  1. Do you have any pain triggers, what makes it better or worse?
    Q - Quality
  2. Can you describe your pain; deep, superficial, crushing, sharp, ache, dull, constant, or intermittent?
    R – Region and Radiation
  1. Can you point to where the pain is?
  2. Does it move or radiate?
    S - Severity
  1. Rate your pain on a 1 to 10 scale with 10 being the worse pain you’ve ever had.
    T - Times
  1. Does the pain prevent you from sleeping, performing ADLs, impact your relationships or work?
  2. Does the pain change during different times of the day?

Assess the Following: (Patient Centered Care)

  1. Moaning, crying, anxiety, grimacing, behavior change, abnormal gait, guarding, diaphoresis, nausea, vomiting, muscle tension, sleep disturbances, restlessness and headache
  2. Inspect site of pain for discoloration, swelling, bleeding or drainage
  3. What has worked for pain control in the past?

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Initiate collaborative pain management plan
  • Provide appropriate nursing pain management techniques
  • Reassessment of pain after intervention

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Outcome of pain assessment
  • Interventions used for pain control
  • Patient’s response to pain interventions
