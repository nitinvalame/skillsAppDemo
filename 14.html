Prerequisite Skills: Concepts of Communication

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Understanding of the importance and variations that can occur fall risk assessment
  • Creates appropriate physical environment
  • Provides privacy
  • Explains procedure
  • Wash hands

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Gather appropriate assessment equipment (chair)

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Create appropriate physical environment
  • Provide privacy
  • Explain procedure
  • Wash hands

Subjective and Objective Data: (Patient Centered Care)

  1. Determine level of consciousness and orientation
  2. Determine if dizziness or vertigo present
  3. History of falls
  4. Alteration in elimination
  5. Delivery of impairment causing pharmacological agents
  6. Ambulatory aid needed
  7. Evaluate muscle strength
  8. Determine gait stability
  9. Evaluate balance
  10. Orthostatic blood pressure assessment
  11. Additional external devices (IV, cast/brace, Foley catheter, etc.) present
  12. Sensory impairment
  13. Mobility impairing disease/conditions

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Provide appropriate nursing management for abnormal findings

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Outcome of assessment
  • Interventions used for nursing management of care
  • Patient’s response to interventions
