Prerequisite Skills: Safe Medication Administration; Aseptic Technique; Intravenous Priming of Tubing

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Prepare medication for one patient at a time
  • Assess the IV site for the presence of inflammation or infiltration
  • Confirm IV tubing for currency
  • Calculate, accurately, the rate of administration, confirming safe dose range
  • Verbalize potential adverse effects and any monitoring requirements
  • Performs 3 checks for safe medication administration
  • Utilizes 2 patient identifiers
  • Evaluates patient allergies

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment
  • Confirm order
  • Gather equipment: IV Piggyback medication, IV administration secondary set, extender hook, labels for tubing, alcohol wipes, IV pole, clean gloves if necessary

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide for privacy
  • Explain procedure
  • Confirm allergies
  • Wash hands

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assess IV site for the presence of infiltration or inflammation
  2. Check medication label with MAR and/or health care provider’s order and verify name, dose, frequency and route.  Check expiration dates and perform calculations. (First check)
  3. Activate piggyback as recommended, if needed
  4. Position primary IV lower than IV piggyback container by placing on extender hook
  5. Check medication label with MAR and/or order (Second check)
  6. If new tubing is used: Remove secondary tubing from package, close regulator clamp.  Aseptically spike the secondary tubing into the IV piggyback container
  7. Clear secondary tubing of air by back flushing.  Cleanse port of primary tubing closest to the primary bag. Connect the secondary line to primary, lower secondary bag below primary bag, open up the regulator clamp and clear tubing of air, filling drip chamber to half full, raise secondary line
  8. If existing tubing is current: Back flush (see step #7) previous medication into old IV piggyback bag and discard old bag.
  9. Check medication label against MAR at the bedside, prior to giving it.  (Third check)
  10. Open the clamp on the secondary tubing and set pump or gravity rate. If using a gravity tubing, control the rate by the primary tubing regulator
  11. Labels tubing with date, time and initials
  12. Dispose of or remove equipment and supplies
  13. Confirm infusion accuracy before leaving room
  14. After medication has finished, close secondary regulator, raise the primary bag and confirm accuracy of the infusion.  Reassess the IV site

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Assessment of insertion site
  • MAR should reflect: Right patient, medication, dose, frequency, and route
  • Patient’s response to procedure
