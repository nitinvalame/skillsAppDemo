Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Complete abdominal assessment with bowel sounds.
  2. Assist the patient to high –Fowler’s position.
  3. Place towel or disposable pad across the patient’s chest.  Give patient basin and tissues.
  4. Ask patient to close one nostril, then the other, and verbalize which one is easier to breathe out of.
  5. Measure the distance to insert tube by placing tip of the tub at the nares, then extending it to tip of earlobe and then to the xiphoid process.
  6. Mark insertion length with marker or tape.
  7. Cut a 4 inch piece of tape and split bottom 2 inches or use a packaged nose tape for NG tubes.
  8. Put on gloves, lubricate the tip of the tube about 3 inches with lubricant.
  9. Ask patient to slightly flex head back against pillow, gently insert tube into the nostril while directing the tube upward and backward along the floor of the nose.
  10. When tube hits the pharynx, instruct patient to touch chin to chest, encouraging sips of water through the straw to facilitate swallowing.
  11. If patient starts of cough or choke, determine if tube has entered the lung and pull back until symptoms subside.
  12. Advance tube until marking is reached.
  13. Remove gloves, secure tube at the bridge of the nose.
  14. Confirm placement of tube using aspiration, air injection or x-ray.
  15. Measure and secure tube.

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assist the patient to the proper positioning for convenience and privacy.
  2. Apply gloves
  3. Disconnect NG from suction or feedings. 
  4. Aspirate stomach contents and replace.
  5. Inject 10-20 mL of air into tube while listening for a “swoosh” of air with stethoscope over stomach.

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Attach tube to feedings or suction, per order
  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Size and type, of catheter.
  • Nostril used for insertion, measurement of tube
  • Patient’s response to procedure including stomach output, content amount, color and characteristics.
