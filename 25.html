Prerequisite Skills: Universal Precautions

Critical Element Criteria:  Students must meet criteria of Critical Elements to safely perform the skill based on evidence-based-practice.  Score can be pass/fail, letter grade or point assignment.  Only acceptable score for Critical Elements is 100%

Critical Elements

  • Maintains Universal Precautions
  • Protects Patient Privacy

Procedure

Prior to Treatment: (Critical Thinking)

  • Assess patients need for treatment
  • Confirm order
  • Gather equipment: Soap and warm  water, soft cloth or gauze, skin preparation/barrier, adhesive, clean pouch with clip, scissors, measuring guide, clean gloves, adhesive tape (if needed)

Patient Interaction: (Safety)

  • Patient identifiers
  • Provide for privacy
  • Explain procedure
  • Confirm allergies
  • Wash hands

Steps of the Procedure (Patient Centered Care; Infection Control)

  1. Assemble equipment on overbed table
  2. Apply gloves
  3. Empty pouch contents and dispose of properly
  4. Remove used bag
  5. Wash the stoma area with soap and water (or other ordered recommendation)
  6. Inspect skin for redness and irritation
  7. Dry stoma and skin with clean gauze
  8. Place skin barrier around stoma
  9. Measure the stoma opening
  10. Cut the opening of the pouch to the proper size
  11. Remove backing from bag, center new bag over stoma and press firmly against skin barrier
  12. Apply pouch clip
  13. Dispose of used pouch appropriately
  14. Remove gloves and wash hands

After Procedure (Patient Centered Care, Safety)

  • Straighten room making patient comfortable, replace call light.
  • Wash hands before leaving room.

Documentation (Informatics, Communication)

  • Assessment of stoma site
  • Patient’s response to procedure
